How it works

The five steps from connection to a working, accountable agent.

Connect your systems

Nien plugs into the software you already run: your ERP or accounting system, CRM, email, chat, and document stores. Nothing is migrated and nothing is copied wholesale. Your data stays where it is. Most connections are live within days, not months.

Set your rules

Your team writes the rules in plain terms: no payment above a threshold without a second approver, this agent may touch these supplier accounts and no others, nothing moves to a bank account you have not seen before. Change a rule and it applies immediately.

Agents get to work, supervised

Agents start in observe and advise mode. They read, draft, match, flag, and propose, and a person signs off on everything. This is where you find out whether an agent is actually good, on your data, at zero risk.

Agents earn autonomy

When an agent's record shows consistent, correct work, you raise its level for that task. Autonomy is granted per task, not wholesale, and dropping an agent back a level takes one action and is effective immediately.

Everything on the record

Every action, approval, and blocked attempt is recorded and signed at the moment it happens. The record cannot be edited after the fact. When your auditor asks what the agents did last quarter, the answer is an export, not an investigation.

Earned autonomy

Agents climb on their record, and can be moved down instantly.

Maybe an agent now pays invoices under your threshold on its own while larger ones still need two approvals. Autonomy is granted per task, not wholesale.
